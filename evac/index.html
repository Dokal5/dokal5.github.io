Executive Commercial Framework

EVAC: Turning Value into Defensible Pricing and Profit Logic

A strategic framework for leaders who need sharper pricing logic, stronger commercial structure, and more disciplined profit capture.

Most firms do not fail because they lack activity. They fail because they cannot clearly translate value into offer structure, pricing architecture, and disciplined commercial action. EVAC helps leadership teams close that gap.

Executive Message

EVAC is built for organizations that want pricing and offer decisions to become more structured, evidence based, and commercially coherent.

System Map

Value capture improves when leaders manage the chain as a system instead of treating price as an isolated number.

Commercial Failure Points

Why commercial decisions break down

These issues rarely appear as isolated pricing problems. They usually reflect a deeper breakdown between value logic, offer structure, evidence, and management discipline.

Five Decision Layers

How EVAC works

EVAC operates as an executive sequence: define value, shape the offer, design the charging logic, validate the price decision, and adapt when commercial conditions change.

System Logic

From value to profit capture

Pricing failure is often a system failure. Weakness at one point in the chain creates downstream pressure on discounting, mix quality, and ultimately profit performance.

Select any point in the chain to trace how upstream logic influences downstream performance and where margin quality usually starts to deteriorate.

Evidence Discipline

What makes a pricing decision defensible

Strong pricing decisions are rarely justified by one input. They become defensible when management combines value logic, market evidence, economics, and strategic intent into one commercial view.

Cross-Model Relevance

How EVAC applies across business models

EVAC is not tied to one industry logic. It helps leadership teams adapt value capture decisions across recurring revenue, physical goods, services, and experience-led businesses.

Strategic Pressure

When leaders need EVAC most

Commercial systems are tested when value perception, costs, or competition shift. Under pressure, weak decision logic shows up quickly and often triggers reactive behavior.

Business Outcomes

What EVAC enables

When value capture decisions become more structured, leadership teams gain clearer commercial logic, faster alignment, and more resilient margin decisions.

Diagnostic View

Where is your commercial architecture weak?

Most symptoms management sees in pricing, discounting, and margin are late-stage effects. The more useful question is where the architecture first became weak.

Integrated View

EVAC in one view

EVAC is most useful when it is treated as a linked decision system. Each layer reinforces the next, improving coherence from value definition through to profit capture.

The framework brings management teams back to five core questions: what customers value, how the offer should be structured, how charging logic should work, how price decisions should be validated, and how the system should adapt when conditions shift.

Define value clearly Anchor the decision system in the customer gains that matter economically and commercially.
Structure the offer coherently Convert value into tiers, bundles, scope boundaries, and trade-offs management can defend.
Design pricing architecture deliberately Choose charging logic that fits the buying logic, usage pattern, and revenue model.
Validate price decisions with evidence Test the decision against willingness to pay, cost boundaries, market context, and strategic intent.
Adapt commercial choices when conditions change Respond with discipline when customer value perception, cost structure, or competition shifts.
EVAC helps firms move from intuitive and fragmented commercial decisions to a more disciplined system for capturing value with stronger logic and greater confidence.
