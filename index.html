About

My work sits at the intersection of pricing, market logic, and education. I am particularly interested in how companies create value, how customers recognize that value, and how pricing and commercial choices shape performance over time.

I teach business with a strong focus on practical reasoning. That means moving beyond abstract concepts and asking sharper questions: What value is actually being created? Why would a customer pay for it? How should a company structure its offer, communicate it clearly, and capture value sustainably?

My teaching and writing often explore pricing, value proposition design, segmentation, positioning, commercial strategy, and decision quality. I am especially interested in helping students and professionals connect strategy with execution in a way that is concrete, measurable, and commercially meaningful.
