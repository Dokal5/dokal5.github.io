Klaviyo and active-profile billing inside retention marketing

Klaviyo looks like a normal martech plan ladder on the surface, but the repaired case is about a narrower question: how the count of emailable active profiles becomes the base billing driver underneath the visible Email and Email + mobile messages packages.

Active-profile billing Reachable audience meter Mobile add-on layer
01 / Pricing Page Visual

Pricing Page Visual

The visible ladder shows channel packaging, but the billing engine underneath it is the active-profile selector.

Pricing artifact

Klaviyo publicly presents Free, Email, and Email + mobile messages, then ties the base price to the size of the emailable active-profile pool inside those packages.

The key thing to notice before scrolling is that the plan names are visible first, while the real bill driver is the reachable audience count beneath them.

Open live pricing page

02 / Core Insight

Core Insight

These three statements map directly to the decision core instead of asking the reader to infer the pricing logic from prose.

  • Klaviyo monetizes the current pool of emailable active profiles rather than all stored customer profiles.
  • The bill changes when more profiles stay emailable and push the account into a higher active-profile band, or when mobile messaging is added.
  • Brands with larger reachable audiences and multichannel retention needs pay more because more of the customer graph stays commercially active.

Monetized: the current emailable audience. Bill changes: when emailable profile count rises or mobile messaging is added. Higher payers: brands that keep more of their customer graph commercially reachable.

03 / Pricing Logic Deconstruction

Pricing Logic Deconstruction

The repaired reading is variable-led: the bill follows an audience count first, then layers channel choice on top.

The bill changes when the count of emailable active profiles rises into a higher paid band or when the buyer adds mobile messaging on top of that audience base.
Key driver Count of emailable active profiles
  • What it does It sets the base plan band that the email subscription has to cover.
Scaling logic Hybrid
  • What it means Audience count is continuous, but the invoice resets upward through discrete active-profile bands.
Main upgrade condition Cross the current active-profile band
  • When it fires More profiles remain emailable before the next billing cycle, or the account adds mobile messaging.
04 / Pricing Matrix / Mechanism Logic

Pricing Matrix / Mechanism Logic

This case uses one primary mechanism only: driver_logic.

Primary component: driver_logic. The visual below keeps the dominant bill driver in the center and treats plan labels and mobile credits as supporting layers.

Driver flow

What feeds the bill

Klaviyo stores all-time customer data, but it only charges the email base on the profiles that still qualify as emailable.

Stored profile layer All-time customer records
  • Billing status Stored data can remain in the account without automatically becoming billable.
Paid driver Emailable active profiles
  • Billing status This count sets the recurring base plan band.
Supporting layer Mobile-message credits
  • Billing status This adds spend only after the active-profile base is already in place.
Bill movement

How the invoice steps up

The commercial sequence is not stored profiles to more stored profiles. It is emailable profiles to a higher band, with optional channel expansion afterward.

Low reach Free
  • Observed boundary Up to 250 active profiles and 500 monthly email sends.
Higher reach Email
  • Observed boundary Base price starts at $45 per month and rises with active-profile bands.
Channel expansion Email + mobile messages
  • Observed boundary Base price starts at $60 per month and adds mobile-message credits on top of the same audience logic.
The dominant mechanism is still driver logic because the base bill follows reachable audience count more directly than it follows the visible plan labels.

06 / Decision Tension Layer

Decision Tension Layer

Each tension below is caused by a named driver, segmentation rule, or upgrade trigger rather than by generic UX discomfort.

Forecasting tension

  • Named driver Count of emailable active profiles.
  • Why it creates friction Marketing teams have to estimate how many profiles will still qualify as emailable before the next cycle closes.

Governance tension

  • Named rule Eligibility and suppression status.
  • Why it creates friction Suppressing dormant profiles becomes a spend-control action, not only a deliverability action.

Channel-bundle tension

  • Named trigger Add SMS or mobile messaging.
  • Why it creates friction The buyer has to decide when multichannel retention deserves a second paid layer on top of the same audience base.
08 / Strategic Opportunity

Strategic Opportunity

The main opening is not a cheaper list price. It is a cleaner link between billing and value than suppression-driven audience eligibility provides today.

Competitor attack angle

Price engaged reach instead of raw emailable reach

Charge for recently engaged subscribers, delivered campaign volume, or another tighter output proxy so stored but dormant profiles do not dominate the pricing story.

Improvement opportunity

Add a clearer inactivity buffer

Use grace periods, explicit dormant-profile exclusions, or simpler audience-state rules so list hygiene does not feel like the buyer's main budgeting lever.
