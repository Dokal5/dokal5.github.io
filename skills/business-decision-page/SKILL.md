# SKILL: Business Decision Logic Page Builder

## Purpose

This skill helps generate or modify webpages that translate business decisions into operational and financial consequences.

This is NOT a teaching page for students.

This is a decision support interface for business decision makers.

The page must help users understand:
- which decision levers they can change
- what those levers influence
- what trade-offs appear
- how changes flow into revenue, cost, and profit

---

## Core Principle

Business decisions change:
1. customer behavior
2. cost structure

These then determine:
- revenue
- margin
- profit

The page must always show cause and effect.

Do not explain concepts academically unless necessary.
Optimize for decision usefulness.

---

## Target Users

- commercial managers  
- marketing managers  
- pricing managers  
- founders  
- business unit leaders  
- product managers  

They care about questions like:
- If price increases, what happens to demand and profit?
- If product improves, can we charge more?
- If we expand channels, what happens to margin?
- Which lever affects which part of the P&L?

---

## Page Must Do

### 1. Decision Selection

User selects a lever:
- Product
- Price
- Promotion
- Place

Then selects direction:
- Increase / Decrease
- Upgrade / Simplify
- Expand / Reduce

---

### 2. Business Impact

Show:
- customer response (WTP, conversion, demand)
- operational impact (cost, complexity)
- competitive effect (positioning, reaction)

---

### 3. Financial Impact

Map directly to:
- Revenue
- COGS
- Gross Margin
- Operating Expenses
- Operating Profit

---

### 4. Trade-Offs

Always show:
- what improves
- what becomes risky

Example:
Price increase:
- ↑ margin per unit
- ↓ potential volume

---

## Page Philosophy

Do NOT describe 4Ps as categories.

Treat them as:
**control variables that reshape demand and cost.**

The page must answer:
- what changes
- why it changes
- where it shows in P&L
- when it fails

---

## Writing Style

Use:
- concise headings
- causal language
- business tone
- practical logic

Avoid:
- academic definitions
- classroom tone
- long explanations without decision relevance

---

## Required Decision Logic

Always express as cause → effect.

### Price
Higher price:
- increases unit margin
- may reduce conversion and volume

Outcome depends on elasticity.

---

### Product
Better product:
- increases perceived value and WTP
- increases cost and complexity

Works only if value is recognized and monetized.

---

### Promotion
More promotion:
- increases awareness and traffic
- increases acquisition cost

Short-term gain vs long-term efficiency risk.

---

### Place
More channels:
- increases reach and convenience
- reduces margin and control

---

## P&L Translation Rules

### Revenue
Changes via:
- volume
- frequency
- price
- basket size

### COGS
Changes via:
- materials
- production
- product specs

### Gross Margin
Changes via:
- price
- cost
- channel mix

### Operating Expenses
Changes via:
- marketing spend
- sales effort
- logistics

### Profit
Depends on:
- balance between revenue and cost
- scalability of decision

---

## Page Structure

1. Header: Decision-focused title  
2. Overview: explain purpose as decision tool  
3. Interactive levers (clickable)  
4. Impact pathway (cause-effect chain)  
5. P&L mapping section  
6. Trade-offs section  
7. Scenario thinking (best / likely / risk)

---

## Interaction Design

Use:
- clickable cards
- tabs
- toggles
- dynamic updates

Every interaction must reveal business logic.

No decorative interaction.

---

## Visual Design

Use:
- clean layout
- structured hierarchy
- clear sections
- visual cause-effect flow

Avoid:
- playful classroom style
- cluttered UI

---

## Output Rules for Code

When generating HTML:

- build single-page interface
- include interactive elements
- update content dynamically (JavaScript)
- show causal logic (not static text)
- include P&L mapping
- keep code modular and readable
- use semantic HTML
- use simple JS (no heavy frameworks unless asked)

---

## What to Avoid

Do NOT:
- build for students
- explain theory without application
- isolate 4Ps
- ignore cost or margin
- assume decisions always improve performance
- say “more promotion = more sales” without conditions

---

## Decision Quality Standard

A good page allows the user to answer:

- What changes if I pull this lever?
- Why does it change?
- Which P&L line is affected?
- What trade-off exists?
- When does this decision fail?

If not, redesign.

---

## Activation Logic

When asked to generate or modify a webpage:

Interpret the task as:

“Build a business decision support interface that connects Product, Price, Promotion, and Place decisions to customer behavior, cost structure, and P&L outcomes through clear cause-effect logic.”

---

## Output Framing

Frame the page as:
- decision map
- business impact tool
- commercial logic interface

Never as:
- teaching material
- lecture content
