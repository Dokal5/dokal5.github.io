Effective Value Capturing

A structured commercial decision system covering value definition, offer design, pricing architecture, evidence discipline, and profit capture under pressure.

Read EVAC 1.0 as a forward sequence: define the value proposition first, translate it into a product and pricing mechanism second, then bring the logic into market decisions.
Step 1
Value Proposition
What is the customer getting, and why is that value meaningful enough to justify commercial attention?
Step 2
Product Configuration
How do we turn that value story into a concrete product, bundle, or service structure?
Step 3
Pricing Mechanism
What charging logic best fits the offer: unit price, bundle, subscription, or another commercial structure?
Step 4
Price and Market Delivery
How do we translate the logic into an actual price and deliver it into a workable market approach?
Read EVAC 2.0 as reverse reasoning under pressure: feasibility first, commercial architecture second, evidence and leverage third, and profit discipline all the way through execution.
Step 1
Target Economics and Capture Boundary
Which cases survive Step 0, and what price-volume-margin corridor is still realistic once constraints become non-negotiable?
Step 2
Offer Design and Corridor Construction
How do we convert the surviving case into an offer, charging logic, and architecture that fit the corridor instead of violating it?
Step 3
Evidence and Power Layer
How do we know the corridor is defensible, and where does leverage actually come from before we enter negotiation?
Step 4
Negotiation and Governance
How do we negotiate inside the corridor, trade concessions intelligently, and stop profit leakage after the launch decision?
