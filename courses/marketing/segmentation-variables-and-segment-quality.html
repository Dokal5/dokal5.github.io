Segmentation Variables and Segment Quality

Teams often know the words demographic, psychographic, and behavioral, but still struggle to tell whether a segmentation is actually useful for strategy.

Core logic
Variable chosen -> Segment revealed -> Strategic usefulness

Variable quality
Actionable groups
Not every way of grouping a market helps a firm make a better decision. Good segmentation reveals differences that are strategically meaningful, not merely easy to describe.
01 · Foundation

A segment should be more than a description

Useful segments help explain differences in need, behavior, economics, or response. Weak segments may still sound neat, but they do not guide targeting, positioning, or the marketing mix very well.

Segmentation variables: who, where, why, how, what

Who Demographic variables such as age, income, occupation, household type, and life stage.
Where Geographic variables such as country, region, city type, climate, and density.
Why Psychographic variables such as lifestyle, identity, aspirations, values, and preferences.
How Behavioral variables such as usage rate, loyalty, occasion, and switching pattern.
What Benefit sought: the specific progress, outcome, or value the customer most wants from the offer.

What makes a segmentation strong

  • It reveals a difference that matters for customer value.
  • It can guide targeting and resource allocation.
  • It suggests different positions, channels, or messages.
  • It is specific enough to act on, not just interesting to observe.

03 · Alignment

Descriptive segments are not automatically strategic segments

The strongest segmentation is the one that visibly changes what a firm should do next.

Weak but familiar

“Women aged 25-34 in cities” sounds clean, but it may still hide very different jobs, budgets, lifestyles, and sought benefits.

  • Easy to describe
  • Harder to infer what message or offer should change
  • Often needs a second layer

Stronger and more actionable

“Busy professionals seeking low-effort weekday meals” already suggests different value, positioning, and channel choices.

  • Explains what progress matters
  • Supports clearer targeting
  • Improves positioning and 4P decisions
