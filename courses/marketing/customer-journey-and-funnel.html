Customer Journey And The Marketing Funnel

A customer journey shows how buyers move from trigger to consideration, evaluation, purchase, postpurchase experience, and loyalty. A marketing funnel shows how the firm responds at each stage with the right channel, message, touchpoint, and campaign logic.

01 · Foundation

Why we need a customer journey

Marketing is not just about sending one message to everyone. Buyers move through different stages, ask different questions, and encounter different touchpoints along the way. A customer journey makes that visible, and the funnel helps the firm decide what to do about it.

What a customer journey is

A customer journey is a stage-by-stage view of how a buyer moves through a decision process. It helps us understand what the buyer is trying to figure out, what influences them, and what touchpoints matter at each moment.

Trigger The buyer encounters a need, problem, or prompt that starts the journey.
Initial Consideration The buyer forms an initial set of brands or options based on prior exposure.
Active Evaluation The buyer adds or subtracts options while searching, comparing, and shopping.
Moment Of Purchase The buyer selects one brand or option at the point of choice.
Postpurchase Experience The buyer experiences the reality of the choice and decides whether to stay in the loop.

Why the funnel belongs here

The customer journey helps us understand the customer's decision logic, key considerations, and relevant touchpoints at each stage. The marketing funnel belongs here because it is the deliberate response we build from that understanding.

In practice, that means a journey map helps us see how implementation should actually work, especially for place and promotion: where we should show up, what channel or touchpoint matters at that stage, and what kind of message or campaign belongs there for that specific purpose.

The larger goal is not only to attract new customers into the journey. It is to keep customers inside the loop after purchase. If the post-purchase experience is strong enough, customers do not fall back into active re-evaluation. They stay, repeat, and become easier to retain than new customers are to acquire.

  • We identify how the customer thinks and what matters at that stage.
  • We identify where the customer is most likely to encounter the brand.
  • We then choose the channels, campaigns, and actions that fit that stage.
  • The goal is alignment: the right response, in the right place, for the right customer logic.
Why We Use It

The objective is not only to map the journey, but to improve it

A customer journey map helps us do more than describe what customers do. It helps us identify where customers move forward, where they get stuck, and where the experience needs to be fixed so the journey becomes more effective for both the customer and the firm.

  • We want to keep customers inside the loyalty loop so they do not fall back into full re-evaluation after purchase.
  • We want to identify exactly where the journey is weak so we know what to improve and how to improve it.
  • We use the map to build the customer experience step by step so it becomes more aligned with customer expectations.
  • That makes our products, services, touchpoints, and messages more relevant at each stage of the journey.

The journey and funnel explain how an individual buyer moves. Product Life Cycle and Diffusion explain what kind of market that buyer is moving inside. Earlier markets usually need more education and trial in the funnel, while later markets usually need more proof, efficiency, retention, and value discipline.

Customer Decision Process · What Customers Think

Trigger

The buyer encounters a need, problem, or prompt that starts the journey. They may not be comparing brands yet, but something has happened that opens the path into consideration.

Decision Logic "Something happened. Do I need to care about this now?"
Touchpoints
  • Life event, need state, or pain point
  • Ambient brand exposure
  • Word of mouth or recent prompts
Main Considerations
  • Problem recognition
  • Relevance
  • Readiness to enter the category
Marketing Funnel · How Do We Respond?

Create relevance and trigger category entry

At this stage, the goal is to make the need legible and make the brand mentally available when the trigger happens.

Funnel Objective Enter the buyer's mind at the moment the trigger appears.
Recommended Channels
  • Broad reach and category cues
  • Top-of-mind branding
  • Problem-led content
Best Campaign Style Trigger-based messaging, need framing, and memorable brand cues.
Why This Match Works The buyer is just entering the journey, so the response should open the path into consideration instead of forcing a decision.
03 · Stage Map

Moments of truth validate whether the McKinsey-style journey and funnel are working

The point of mapping the customer journey is not only to describe stages. It is to design a funnel response that helps us succeed at the key moments of truth. If those moments are not working, the journey design and the funnel response need improvement.

Click A Stage · How Do We Help Customers Pass It?

Trigger

We help customers pass this stage by making the brand easy to notice, easy to remember, and easy to connect to the need that just appeared.

Focus on category cues, problem framing, and broad-reach touchpoints that put the brand into the buyer's mind before comparison begins.

Tactics To Prioritize
  • SEO and search ads around emerging needs
  • Problem-led content and social distribution
  • Memorable brand cues across high-reach media
Examples
  • Publish a landing page that answers “How do I solve this problem?” before pitching the offer.
  • Run YouTube or Instagram ads that frame the pain point and link it to the category.
  • Build remarketing audiences from educational content viewers for the next stage.
