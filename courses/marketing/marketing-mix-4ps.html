Marketing Mix As The Translation Of Value

Product, price, place, and promotion do not create value on their own. They translate a chosen value logic into four different market decisions so the position becomes real.

Marketing Mix
One value idea can appear in four different forms: what it is, what it costs, where it appears, and how it is understood.
01 · Foundation

The 4Ps start with the value people are actually buying

Before deciding product, price, place, or promotion, the firm has to know what kind of progress matters to the customer. The marketing mix is the implementation layer that makes that value visible and accessible in the market.

The source of the value comes first

People do not buy the offer just because it exists. They buy progress: saved time, reduced risk, more confidence, better performance, stronger identity, or a smoother system. That source logic comes first. The 4Ps come after.

Job What is the person trying to get done, solve, avoid, or enable?
Value What kind of improvement is decisive here: functional, economic, psychological, social, systemic, or risk reduction?
Position What should the offer mean to the chosen audience because of that value?

The 4Ps should follow the customer profile

Once the value logic is clear, each part of the marketing mix should respond to something specific we know about the customer. The 4Ps are not abstract decisions. They should be shaped by the benefit sought, affordability, touchpoints, and message resonance of the audience.

Product Build around the benefit the customer is actually looking for.
Price Set it in line with what this audience can afford and what they are willing to pay for that benefit.
Place Show up in the channels, locations, and touchpoints where this audience already appears.
Promotion Use messages, language, and proof that this audience can actually recognize and resonate with.

Product Translation

Turn the value into the experience people actually use

Product gives the chosen value a tangible form. If the value is "less friction," the offer should feel simpler, faster, and easier to operate from the first interaction onward.

Purpose Deliver the value through the design of the offer itself.
Translation Question What should the product be like if this value claim is true?
Concrete Example Fast onboarding, fewer configuration steps, intuitive navigation, and responsive support.
Customer Profile Link This audience values ease, reliability, and time-saving benefits, so the offer should be built around those expected gains.
Position Supported Built for people who do not have time for avoidable complexity.
03 · Alignment

A position becomes believable only when the 4Ps agree

The 4Ps are not separate tactics. Together, they form the market expression of a single value logic.

Aligned example

A company wants to stand for reliable speed for busy professionals.

All four support the same meaning
  • Product: minimal setup and dependable performance
  • Price: premium enough to signal time-saving value
  • Place: direct online access and quick onboarding
  • Promotion: fewer delays, less friction, more focus

Misaligned example

The same company claims reliability for professionals but acts like a discount commodity brand.

One P breaks the position
  • Product: polished and efficient
  • Price: extreme discount signal
  • Place: crowded low-trust channels
  • Promotion: cheap, basic, bargain messaging
