Marketing

A structured marketing decision system covering segmentation, positioning, messaging, execution, and performance so organizations can make sharper commercial choices.

Course Sequence Map

This sequence is designed for real commercial decisions. First, leaders need a better understanding of what customers value and how demand is structured. Then they need to choose where to compete and how to position. Only after that should they translate strategy into execution. Finally, they need to evaluate whether those decisions are producing the right outcomes and how priorities should change.

Read this map as a management logic chain: diagnosis first, strategic choice second, execution third, performance review last.
Part 1
Market Understanding
Meaning: clarify what customers value, how demand differs, and how the market is structured before making competitive commitments.
Part 2
Strategic Choice
Meaning: decide which customers matter most, what position the firm should occupy, and what competitive direction should guide resource allocation.
Part 3
Marketing Execution
Meaning: convert strategy into concrete choices in offer, channels, touchpoints, and customer journey so the market can actually experience the position.
Part 4
Performance and Evolution
Meaning: assess whether the strategy is working, what economics it produces, and how priorities should evolve as customer behavior and market conditions change.
