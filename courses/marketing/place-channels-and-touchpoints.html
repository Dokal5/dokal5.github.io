Place, Channels, and Touchpoints

Decision makers often hear “place” and think only of location. In practice, place is about how access is created, which channels move the offer, and where the customer actually encounters the brand.

A distribution channel is how the offer reaches the customer. A touchpoint is where the customer experiences the brand. Those two often overlap, but they are not the same thing.
01 · Foundation

Place is really about access and availability

Good channel design makes the offer easy to find, easy to get, and easy to trust in the context where the customer wants it.

Three terms to separate

Place The broad access logic: how the offer becomes available in the market.
Channel The route through which the offer is delivered or sold, such as direct, retail, marketplace, or partner.
Touchpoint The specific moment where the customer interacts with the brand, platform, seller, or service experience.

Why the distinction matters

  • A channel decision shapes margins, control, and reach.
  • A touchpoint decision shapes trust, clarity, and experience.
  • The best channel is not always the one with the most reach.
  • The best touchpoint sequence depends on the customer's journey stage.

03 · Alignment

A good channel strategy matches both the offer and the customer logic

The same offer can feel premium, convenient, confusing, or generic depending on how access is designed.

Aligned route

  • Premium, explanation-heavy offers often benefit from more controlled touchpoints.
  • Routine, low-involvement offers may benefit from broad access and low-friction availability.
  • Channel and message should reinforce the same position.

Misaligned route

  • A premium promise can be weakened by low-trust or cluttered sales environments.
  • A convenience promise can fail if access is slow or fragmented.
  • The wrong touchpoints can create doubt even when the product is strong.
